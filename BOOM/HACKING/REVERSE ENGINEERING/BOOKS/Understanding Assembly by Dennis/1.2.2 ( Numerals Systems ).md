
This section explores how computers represent numbers compared to humans.

- **Decimal**: The system humans are most accustomed to, likely because we have 10 fingers, though "10" has no special scientific significance.
- **Binary**: The natural system for digital electronics, where 0 represents the absence of current and 1 represents its presence. It has a **radix** (base) of 2.
- **Hexadecimal**: Widely used in computing to emphasize the **bit pattern** of a number rather than its numeric value.
- **Octal**: Currently used almost exclusively for file permissions on POSIX systems.

The author emphasizes that while the notation changes between systems, the **value of the number remains the same**.
